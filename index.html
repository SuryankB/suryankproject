Some Waste Facts

Food Waste Is a Major Contributor Climate Change:

Globally, about one-third of all food produced goes to waste. This wasted food contributes 8-10% of total greenhouse gas emissions, mainly from the decomposition of organic waste in landfills, which produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas

Discovery of Plastic-Eating Bacteria:

In 2016, Japanese scientists discovered a bacterium named Ideonella sakaiensis, which can break down PET plastic, commonly used in bottles and containers.These enzymes could revolutionize recycling by enabling a circular economy where plastics are efficiently broken down and reused. This could dramatically reduce plastic waste, especially hard-to-recycle plastics, which often end up in landfills or incinerated.

Some Countries Are Almost Completely Waste-Free:

Sweden, for instance, has developed a near zero-waste system. They recycle about 99% of their household waste and use much of it to produce energy, resulting in minimal landfill usage.
